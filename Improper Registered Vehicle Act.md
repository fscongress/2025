*Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the State of Firestone in Congress assembled,*

**SECTION 1. TITLE**

This bill may be known as the “Improper Registered Vehicle Act”.

**SEC. 2. EFFECTIVENESS**

This Act shall be effective upon completion of the constitutionally required process.

**SEC. 3. SEVERABILITY**

Should any part of this Act be struck down or declared unenforceable, then that part of the Act shall be considered severed from this Act, and the rest shall remain in full force.

**SEC. 4. DEFINITIONS**

For the purposes of this act–

(1) “Criminal Code” means definition [Trello](https://trello.com/b/EGN3OQzQ/firestone-criminal-code).

**SEC. 5. IMPROPER REGISTERED VEHICLE ACT**

(a) Chapter 4, Section 29 of the Firestone Criminal Code shall be established and be titled,
“§29 – Driving with an Improper License Plate.” It shall be recognized as a Class C Misdemeanor carrying a jail time of 120-200 seconds. It shall state:

"(a) The act of Driving with an Improper License Plate shall be recognized as an unlawful criminal offense within the State of Firestone.

(b) Driving an Improperly Registered Vehicle shall be defined as driving a vehicle with a license plate where the registration does not match the vehicle’s actual description."
